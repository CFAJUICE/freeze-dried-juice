Building a more complicated formula

You are reading an ad for a new cell phone data plan. It says "Save big on cell phone service for only $39.99 per month! This includes 300 phone minutes, 300 text messages, and 3 gigabytes of data. Additional costs are $0.35 for each additional minute, $0.05 for each additional text and $12 for each additional gigabyte.

Each month you talk on the phone for 400 minutes, send 500 text messages, and use 4 gigabytes of data. If you currently pay $119.99 per month, could you save money on this plan?

Summarize what you know

Here's what you know about the plan:
Base cost = $39.33 per month
Included in the plan is: 300 minutes, 300 texts, 3 gigabytes of data
Additional costs are: $0.35 per minute, $0.05 per text and $12 per gigabyte of data

Write a math expression for the plan cost in words

Think about the total cost of the plan. It is made up of the cost for the minutes, text, and data. The math expression in words is:
Total cost = Base Cost + Cost of Minutes more than 300 + Cost of Texts more than 300 + Cost of Data more than 3

Choose letters to represent the values that vary

The values that vary each month are the number of minutes more than 300, the number of texts more than 300, and the amount of data over 3 gigabytes. You choose the letters M to represent minutes more than 300, T to represent texts more than 300, and D to represent data more than 3 gigabytes.

Translate the words in the math expression to math

Use what you know about the plan and the letters for the amounts that vary to translate the math expression from words into math.
Total cost = Base Cost + Cost of Minutes more than 300 + Cost of Texts more than 300 + Cost of Data more than 3
Base Cost is 39.99. Cost of Minutes more than 300 is 0.035 times M. Cost of Texts more than 300 is 0.05 times T. Cost of Data more than 3 gigabytes is 12 times D.
The math expression for Total Cost is 39.99 + 0.035M + 0.05T + 12D

Figuring out the values for the letters

In each case, your usage is larger than or greater than the amount allowed by the plan. To figure out the values for M, T, and D, you need to subtract the amount allowed by the plan from your usage.
M = your usage minus 300 which equals 400 minus 300 = 100
T = your usage minus 300 which equals 500 minus 300 = 200
D = your usage minus 3 which equals 4 minus 3 = 1

Substitute into the formula and do the math

Total cost = 39.99 + 0.35M + 0.05T + 12D
Total cost = 39.99 + 0.35 times 100 + 0.05 times 200 + 12 times 1
Total cost = 39.99 + 35 + 10 + 12
Total cost = 96.99

Make sure your answer makes sense and summarize what you have found including your decision:

Think this: $96.99 seems like a reasonable amount for a monthly cell phone charge with a base cost of $39.99.
You state what you have found: "This is less expensive than my current plan, which costs me $119.99 per month, so I will save money if I switch!"
