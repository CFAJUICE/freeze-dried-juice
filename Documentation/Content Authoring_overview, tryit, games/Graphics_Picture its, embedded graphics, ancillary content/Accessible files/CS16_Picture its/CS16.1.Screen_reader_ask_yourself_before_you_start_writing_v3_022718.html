Ask yourself: Before you start writing

Ask yourself, what is my purpose?

My purpose is to share information.

This means that I should think about what my readers want to know. My purpose is to share clear, factual information; not to offer opinions.

My purpose is to tell a story.

This means that I should decide what the story is about and what parts will be the most interesting to my readers. My purpose is to describe events that happened in the past. I need to tell them in the order they occurred.

My purpose is to convince someone.

This means that I should think about what I am arguing for and what my readers might believe. My purpose is to persuade them that my ideas are correct.

Ask yourself, who is my audience?

My audience does not know much about my topic.

This means that my writing should include background information that explains the topic, information that will interest my readers, and it should let my readers know that my ideas are important.

My audience are experts in my topic.

This means that my writing should include accurate details, new ideas, and analysis of the topic.

My audience may not agree with me.

This means that my writing should include details that will convince readers that I am correct, it should acknowledge their point of view, and should include new ideas.
