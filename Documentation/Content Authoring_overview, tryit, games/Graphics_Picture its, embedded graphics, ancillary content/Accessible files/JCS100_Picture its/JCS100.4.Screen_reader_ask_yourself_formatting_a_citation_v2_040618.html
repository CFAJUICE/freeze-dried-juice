Ask yourself: Deciding how to format a citation

Your in-text citations will be formatted differently, depending on the writing that surrounds them.

To decide how to format a citation, ask yourself whether or not you are introducing the borrowed material with a signal phrase. Then ask yourself if the borrowed material is a direct quotation. Your answers to these two questions will tell you how to format your citation!

You are introducing your information with a signal phrase.

Include the year of publication in parentheses after the author's name, followed by a comma.
Example: According to Edgar Smith open parentheses 2017 close parentheses, comma.
Edgar Smith is the author's name and the year of publication is 2017.

Your signal phrase introduces a direct quotation

Also include the page or paragraph number after the direct quotation and before the period. Use P period for a page number or p a r a period for a paragraph number.
Example: According to Edgar Smith open parentheses 2017 close parentheses comma, open quotes When ants are on the move, they create a pheromone trail for other ants to follow close quotes open parentheses P period 4 close parentheses period.

You are not introducing your information with a signal phrase.

Include the author's last name and year of publication in parentheses at the end of the borrowed material.
Example: Ants play follow the leader by following a trail made of pheromones open parentheses Smith comma 2017 close parentheses period.

You are using a direct quotation

Also include the page or paragraph number after the year of publication in the citation. Use P period for a page number or p a r a period for a paragraph number.
Example: Open quotes When ants are on the move, they create a pheromone trail for other ants to follow close quotes open parentheses Smith comma 2017 comma P period 4 close parentheses period.
