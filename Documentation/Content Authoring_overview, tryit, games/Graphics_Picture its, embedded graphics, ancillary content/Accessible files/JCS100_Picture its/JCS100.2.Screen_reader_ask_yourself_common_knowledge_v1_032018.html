Ask yourself: Deciding if information is common knowledge

Facts that are widely known or easy to find in general sources are considered to be common knowledge. To decide if information is common knowledge, ask yourself:

Who is my audience?

Next, ask yourself:

Is my information something that they would be likely to know?

If you answer yes, your information is common knowledge and does not need to be cited.

If you answer no, next ask yourself:

Is my information something they can find easily from general sources?

If you answer yes, your information is common knowledge and does not need to be cited.

If you answer no, your information is not common knowledge. It needs to be cited!
