Summarizing

When you use the main ideas from an original source and use your own words to explain what you mean, you are summarizing. In your summary, you include the main ideas from the original source, but none of the details. Your summary will be considerably shorter than the original.

Original source material from Learning About a Vehicle's History by D. Francise, published in 2015:

The first thing you want to do is ensure that the car has not been stolen. Armed with the VIN, visit the National Insurance Crime Bureau's database at www.nicb.org. Click on "VIN Check," and enter the VIN. The database keeps records for the past five years, and it will alert you if the vehicle has been stolen. It will also provide information about any police reports that are related to the car.

Main ideas and details

The main ideas are: "Visit the National Insurance Crime Bureau to find out if the car has been stolen" and "NICB also provides information about police reports." You will include these main ideas in your summary.

The details are: "Use the VIN (vehicle identification number)", "Click on VIN check", and "Database keeps records for 5 years." You will not include these details in your summary.
