Organizing ideas in an outline

When you organize the main idea and details of a paragraph in an outline, you list the paragraph's main idea as a heading labeled with a letter, and include the details in a numbered list below it. When you are outlining an article with more than one paragraph, you repeat this organization for each paragraph.

Example 1: Soccer, the sport for everyone!

Read this paragraph and decide what the main idea and supporting details are: Soccer is a sport enjoyed around the world because it is accessible to almost everyone. Soccer can be played anywhere: on a field, in a street, or in a schoolyard. In terms of equipment, all that is needed is a ball. Soccer players may wear cleats, sneakers, sandals, or simply play in bare feet. Teams may be made up of any number of players as long as the two teams are equal in number.

The main idea is that soccer is enjoyed around the world because it is accessible to almost everyone. The supporting details are that soccer can be played anywhere, the only equipment required is a ball, players may wear anything on their feet, teams may be of any size, and just need to be equal in number.

The outline for this paragraph is this:

A. Soccer is enjoyed around the world because it is accessible to almost everyone.

1. Soccer can be played anywhere.
2. The only equipment required is a ball.
3. Players may wear anything on their feet.
4. Teams may be of any size, and just need to be equal in number.

Example 2: The magic of music

Read this paragraph and decide what the main idea and supporting details are: Music helps keep non-academic kids in school. Kids have different interests and learning styles. Some young people do not respond to traditional math and reading instruction, but find a true connection with music. These students are more likely to stay in school if they can be doing something they love while attending school. For some, music might lead to a career.

The main idea is that music helps keep non-academic kids in school. The supporting details are that some kids do not respond to traditional courses, they find a connection with music, these students may stay in school if they can study the subject they love, and studying music in school might lead to a career.

The outline for this paragraph is this:

A. Music helps keep non-academic kids in school.

1. Some kids do not respond to traditional courses.
2. They find a connection with music.
3. These students may stay in school if they can study the subject they love.
4. Studying music in school might lead to a career.
