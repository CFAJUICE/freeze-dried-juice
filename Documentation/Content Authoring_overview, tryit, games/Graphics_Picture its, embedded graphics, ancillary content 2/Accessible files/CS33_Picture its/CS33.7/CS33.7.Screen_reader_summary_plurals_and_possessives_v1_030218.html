Plurals and possessives: Putting it all together

Plural nouns

Plural nouns describe more than one of a person, place, or thing. Example: There are many cats in the house next door.

How to form a plural noun

There are several ways to form plural nouns. Check a dictionary if you are not sure.
For many nouns, add an S to the singular form. Example: The plural form of "cat" is C A T + S, or "cats."
For some nouns, add an E S to the singular form. Example: The plural form of "match" is M A T C H + E S, or "matches."
If the noun ends in Y, remove the Y and add I E S. Example: The plural form of "beauty" is B E A U T I E S, or "beauties."
If the noun is irregular, check a dictionary. Example: The plural form of "mouse" is M I C E, or "mice."
Careful! Never use an apostrophe to form a plural noun.

Possessive nouns

Possessive nouns show ownership. Example: The cats' favorite food is fish.

How to form a possessive noun

If the noun is singular, add an apostrophe + S. Example: The possessive form of "cat" is C A T apostrophe S, or "cat's."
If the noun is plural and irregular, add an apostrophe + S. Example: The possessive form of "mice " is M I C E apostrophe S, or "mice's."
If the noun is plural and ends in an s, and an apostrophe by itself. Example: The possessive form of "cats" is C A T S apostrophe, or "cats'."
Careful! Do not add an S after the apostrophe for a plural noun.

Possessive pronouns

Possessive pronouns such as "his," "her," or "its" also show ownership. Example: The cats hunt in his yard.
Possessive pronouns do not use an apostrophe.
Careful! Do not use an apostrophe with the possessive form of I T, or "it." The possessive form of "it" is I T S, or "its."
