When to use capital letters: Putting it all together

Here is a summary to help you remember when to use capital letters

Basics of capitalization

Always capitalize the first word in a sentence

An example is, "Regular exercise is important for everyone." The "R" in "regular" is capitalized.

Always capitalize the pronoun I

An example is, "Some days I walk to work." The pronoun "I" is capitalized.

People, places, and things

Use a capital letter when naming specific people, places, or things

One example is, "Let's go on a bike ride with Dad." Dad is capitalized in this sentence because it is referring to a specific person.

Another example is, "The traffic to Harper Beach was unbearable." Harper Beach is capitalized because it is referring to a specific place.

The last example is, "There is a special exhibit at the Smith Museum." Smith Museum is capitalized because it is referring to a specific place.

Do not use a capital letter when naming people, places, or things in general.

One example is, "My dad rides his bike every week." Dad is not capitalized in his sentence because it can apply to many persons.

Another example is, "The traffic to the beach was unbearable." Beach is not capitalized in this sentence because it could be referring to many beaches.

The last example is, "There is a special exhibit at the museum." Museum is not capitalized in this sentence because it could be referring to many museums.

Remember, don't use capital letters to add emphasis.

Do's and don'ts

Do capitalize:

Names of the months. For example, "Last year, we got snow in May." May is capitalized.

Days of the week. For example, "I get lunch with my friends every Wednesday." Wednesday is capitalized.

Languages. For example, "My school offered Spanish and French classes." Spanish and French are both capitalized.

Course names. For example, "Every student is required to take Sociology 101." Sociology 101 is capitalized.

Specific animal breeds. For example, "The Labrador Retriever is a popular breed." Labrador Retriever is capitalized.

The first word, last word, and all important words of books, movies, songs, or articles. One example is, "Last week I read, All Quiet on the Western Front." The words all, quiet, western, and front are capitalized. Another example is, "The film, Singin' in the Rain, is a classic." The words singin' and rain are both capitalized.

Do not capitalize:

Names of seasons. For example, "My favorite season is summer." Summer is not capitalized.

Types of animals. For example, "The most popular pets are dogs and cats." Dogs and cats are not capitalized.

Subjects studied unless they are languages. For example, "My highest grades were in math and science." Math and science are not capitalized.
