Three ways to fix a run-on sentence

There are three ways to fix a run-on sentence. You can create two shorter sentences, add a comma and a joining word between the two clauses, or add a semicolon between the two clauses. Here are examples of each technique using the run-on sentence, "Training benefits every dog it also creates a bond between dog and owner."

Make two shorter sentences

You do this by putting a period after "Training benefits every dog" and capitalizing the first word of the next sentence, "it." Now the sentences are, "Training benefits every dog period. It also creates a bond between dog and owner."

Add a comma and a joining word

You do this by putting a comma after "Training benefits every dog" and adding the joining word, "and", after the comma. Now the sentence is, "Training benefits every dog comma, and it also creates a bond between dog and owner."

Add a semicolon

You do this by putting a semicolon after "Training benefits every dog." Now the sentence is, "Training benefits every dog semicolon; it also creates a bond between dog and owner."
