The 8 parts of speech

Part What it does Examples
Noun Names a person, place, thing, or idea. The wrestler stepped into the ring.   "Wrestler" is a person and "ring" is a thing. They are both nouns.
Pronoun Takes the place of a noun. He stepped into the ring.   "He" takes the place of the noun wrestler. It is a pronoun.
Verb Expresses the action or condition of a noun. The team jumped up and down. The fruitcake is delicious.  "Jumped" tells what action the team is taking, and "is" tells something about the condition of the fruitcake. They are both verbs.
Adjective Describes a noun or pronoun. The frisky puppy wagged his tail.  "Frisky" is describing the puppy. It is an adjective.
Adverb Modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb. The ballerina danced slowly. The kids were very happy when it snowed. Tim paints extremely well.  "Slowly" tells how the ballerina was dancing, "very" tells how happy the kids were, and "extremely" tells how well Tim paints. They are all adverbs.
Preposition Relates a noun or pronoun to another word in the sentence. The bear went over the mountain. We are going to the grocery store.  "Over" relates the bear and the mountain. We and the grocery store are related by the word "to". "Over" and "to" are both prepositions.
Conjunction Connects words, groups of words, or sentences. The smartphone and the tablet are both small enough to carry everywhere.  "And" connects smartphone and the tablet. It is a conjunction.
Interjection Expresses excitement or strong feeling. Ouch! The frying pan is still hot.  "Ouch" is an interjection.
