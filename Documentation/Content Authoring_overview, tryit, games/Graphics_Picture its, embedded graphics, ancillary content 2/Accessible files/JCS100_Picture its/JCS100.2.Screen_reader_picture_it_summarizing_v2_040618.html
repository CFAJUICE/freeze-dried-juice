Summarizing

When you use the main ideas from an original source and use your own words to explain what you mean, you are summarizing. In your summary, you include the main ideas from the original source, but none of the details. Your summary will be considerably shorter than the original.

Original source material from Coney Island: The Amusement Park as Urban Symbol in American Art by R. Cox, published in 1979:

During the 1930s, Coney Island in New York City was a haven for city folks who wanted to escape the oppressive summer heat. New Yorkers flocked to the beach to cool off in the waves and socialize with friends and family. For working class people, this day excursion provided an opportunity to squeeze some leisure time into a long work week.

Main ideas and details

The main ideas are: "in the 1930's, New York city residents treated Coney Island as a haven (retreat or oasis)" and "Day excursions allowed people to fit leisure time into their work week." You will include these main ideas in your summary.

The details are: "escape from summer heat", "cool off in the waves", and "socialize with friends and family." You will not include these details in your summary.
