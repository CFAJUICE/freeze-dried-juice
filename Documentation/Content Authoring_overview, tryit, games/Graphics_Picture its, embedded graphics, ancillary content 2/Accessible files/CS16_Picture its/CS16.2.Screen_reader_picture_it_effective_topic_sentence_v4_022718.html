Effective topic sentence

Too narrow

A narrow topic sentence has too much information or information that is too detailed.

An example of a topic sentence that is too narrow is, "My stay at your motel last month was a terrible experience for my family and me because of the uncomfortable beds."

This topic sentence is too narrow because you plan to write about more than just the uncomfortable beds. It is like a chair with one leg; it doesn't balance with your paragraph.

Too broad

A broad topic sentence is too general and doesn't have enough information.

An example of a topic sentence that is too broad is, "I stayed at your motel last month."

This topic sentence is too broad because it doesn't help your reader understand what you will write about. It is like a chair with too many legs; there are too many potential topics and no focus.

Just right

An effective topic sentence will have enough information to narrow your topic and help your reader understand what you will write about in this paragraph.

An example of an effective topic sentence is, "My stay at your motel last month was a terrible experience for my family and me because of the poor quality of the motel and the poor service.

This topic sentence is effective because it tells your reader everything you will be writing about. It is like a well-balanced chair with four legs.
