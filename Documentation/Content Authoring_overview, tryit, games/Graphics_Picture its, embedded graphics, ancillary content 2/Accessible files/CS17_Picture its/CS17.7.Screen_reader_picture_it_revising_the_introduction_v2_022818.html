Revising an essay introduction

You're revising the introductory paragraph of your essay to make sure that it introduces your topic in an engaging way, has a clear purpose, and contains a thesis statement that clearly states what you believe.

First draft

The first sentence of your introduction is, "It is a really good idea to learn to play a musical instrument." You realize this isn't very engaging and decide to change it.

The second sentence is, "I have been trying to learn guitar for a few years now." You decide this is too personal to be good background information on your topic.

The last sentence is, "Playing music will be one of the best moves you ever make." This is a thesis statement giving your opinion, but it doesn't show a clear purpose or plan for the rest of the essay.

Revised draft

The new first sentence is, "Imagine yourself surrounded by friends, playing the musical instrument of your choice, and leading an energetic sing-along." This introductory sentence is engaging and creates a picture of what you hope to share.

The new second sentence is, "Learning to play a musical instrument may require a commitment of time, energy, and money, but the end result will guarantee numerous benefits for you." Now the background information lets the reader know what goes into learning a musical instrument.

The new thesis statement is, "Learning to read music, sing, and play an instrument encourages you to entertain friends and family, improves your ability to focus on a task, and provides a relief from the hectic day-to-day responsibilities." This thesis statement tells the reader why you believe learning to play an instrument is a good idea. It clarifies your purpose for writing, lists ideas in order of importance, and presents a plan for the rest of the essay.

The revised draft of your introduction reads: Imagine yourself surrounded by friends, playing the musical instrument of your choice, and leading an energetic sing-along. Learning to play a musical instrument may require a commitment of time, energy, and money, but the end result will guarantee numerous benefits for you. Learning to read music, sing, and play an instrument encourages you to entertain friends and family, improves your ability to focus on a task, and provides a relief from the hectic day-to-day responsibilities.
