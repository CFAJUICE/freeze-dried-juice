Subject-verb agreement: Putting it all together

Sentence has one subject

If the subject is a singular noun, use a singular verb. Example: The cat sleeps in the sun.
If the subject is a plural noun, use a plural verb. Example: The cats sleep in the sun.
If the noun can't be counted, use a singular verb. Example: The juice is very fresh.
Did you know? Singular regular verbs end in an s, like sleeps. Plural regular verbs do not, like sleep.

Compound subjects: Subjects separated by "and" or "or"

If subjects are separated by "and", use a plural verb. Example: Kim and Dave ride the bus.
If the subjects are separated by "or", match the noun that is closest to the verb. Example: Soups or salad comes with your meal.
Another example: Salad or soups come with your meal.

Indefinite pronouns as subjects: Anybody, both, each, etc.

If the pronoun is singular, use "is." Example: Anybody is welcome to try the pie or cake. Each is delicious. Note! The pronoun "Each" is always singular. Pronouns that begin with "every" or "any" are also singular.
If the pronoun is plural, use "are." Example: Both are made here at the bakery. Many are tempted to try them.

Subject is a collective noun or group

If the collective noun is acting as a unit, use a singular verb. Example: The committee holds regular meetings.
If the collective noun is a group acting as individuals, use a plural verb. Example: The committee decide how to vote by reviewing the data.
Note! If a group is acting as individuals, a writer will often phrase the sentence to make this clear. The committee members decide how to vote by reviewing the data.

Tricky situations

If the subject is separated from the verb, find the subject and match it! Example: The choice of campsites is limited. The subject is "choice," which is singular, so the verb is singular.
Another example: The possibilities for a miserable night are endless. The subject is "possibilities," which is plural, so the verb is plural. Don't be misled by the intervening words!
To decide whether to start a sentence with "There is" or"There are," find the subject of the sentence and match it! Example: There are bears near the camp. The subject is "bears" so use the plural, "There are."
Another example: There is a cub with one of the bears. The subject is "cub" so use the singular, "There is."
You can try rephrasing the sentence with the subject first to decide. Example: Bears are near the camp. A cub is with one of the bears.
