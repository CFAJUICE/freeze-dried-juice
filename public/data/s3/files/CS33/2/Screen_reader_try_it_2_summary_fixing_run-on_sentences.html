Three ways to fix a run-on sentence

There are three ways to fix a run-on sentence. You can create two shorter sentences, add a comma and a joining word between the two clauses, or add a semicolon between the two clauses. Here are examples of each technique using the run-on sentence, "Many cat owners are puzzled by their cats' behavior they don't understand why cats purr."

Make two shorter sentences

You do this by putting a period after "Many cat owners are puzzled by their cats' behavior" and capitalizing the first word of the next sentence, "they." Now the sentences are, "Many cat owners are puzzled by their cats' behavior period. They don't understand why cats purr."

Add a comma and a joining word

You do this by putting a comma after "Many cat owners are puzzled by their cats' behavior" and adding the joining word, "and", after the comma. Now the sentence is, "Many cat owners are puzzled by their cats' behavior comma, and they don't understand why cats purr."

Add a semicolon

You do this by putting a semicolon after "Many cat owners are puzzled by their cats' behavior." Now the sentence is, "Many cat owners are puzzled by their cats' behavior semicolon; they don't understand why cats purr."
