Ask yourself: Deciding if you have shown possession correctly

To decide if you have used the correct possessive form of a noun:

First, ask yourself what kind of noun is it?

Is it a singular noun (like dog or bus); an irregular plural noun that does not end in S (like children); or a plural noun that ends in S (like cats)?

Singular noun (like dog or bus)

Next, ask yourself have I added apostrophe S to the end of the noun, like D O G apostrophe S dog's, or B U S apostrophe S bus's?

If you answer no, you have not shown possession correctly.

If you answer yes, you have shown possession correctly!

Irregular plural noun that does not end in S (like children)

Next, ask yourself have I added an apostrophe S to the end of the noun, C H I L D R E N apostrophe S children's?

If you answer no, you have not shown possession correctly.

If you answer yes, you have shown possession correctly!

Plural noun that ends in S (like cats)

Next, ask yourself have I added only an apostrophe after the last S, like C A T S apostrophe cats'?

If you answer no, you have not shown possession correctly.

If you answer yes, you have shown possession correctly!
