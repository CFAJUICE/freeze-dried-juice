What is JUICE?

Welcome to JUICE interactive and personalized skill-building resources. JUICE was funded by a U.S. Department of Education grant and is available to students at partnering academic institutions. JUICE gives you a choice of different ways to review and practice important academic skills while you are working on college projects and assignments. “JUICE” stands for “just-in-time, contextualized, and empowering” academic support.

Your online course may link directly to a specific JUICE skill-building module or link to the JUICE Home page where you can browse or search for help on different kinds of skills, including writing, mathematics, and others. Each JUICE module supports a group of related skills and helps you find the specific skill you need to review. For example, there's a JUICE module on how to write an essay in which each mini-lesson focuses on one skill, such as how to write a good thesis statement.

JUICE always uses real-world situations to help you review, practice and, remember how to apply skills.

From a JUICE module page, start with the page tour, then explore mini-lessons on different skills, or play the Challenge Wheel game to find out what you need to review.

To play a Challenge Wheel game:

  • Click the Challenge Wheel box on the module page.
  • Play all the rounds.
  • Review the Scoreboard to find out what you need to practice. You can go to any mini-lesson in the module from the Scoreboard.
  • JUICE saves your Scoreboard and you can return to it by clicking the Challenge Wheel box again.

To work in a mini-lesson, click the mini-lesson box and start with any one of these choices:

  • Read and listen to a self-paced Overview on the basics.
  • Choose a step-by-step Try It example that walks you through how to apply the skill with detailed feedback and hints.
  • Play a short Challenge Game with hints, feedback, a Show Answer option, and cheers when you get to the end!

JUICE support for accessibility

The main JUICE site pages and the Overview and Try it sections of each mini-lesson support keyboard navigation and screen readers. JUICE Challenge games and some of the other site pages are not yet accessible.

IMPORTANT! JUICE keyboard navigation assumes that the user will be using the tab key on both Windows and the Mac to move from one area of the page to the next. Keyboard navigation has not been optimized for other forms of screen navigation.

Getting started with JUICE

JUICE typically opens on a module page where you can choose from a set of related skills. Your online course or resource area might also link to the JUICE Home page, where you can browse through all the modules in the library. Wherever you start, you can always use Home or Search to find help. Here are few tips on JUICE module pages:

  • Use the Page Tour (upper right in the green banner) for a quick introduction.
  • Try playing the Challenge Wheel game. It's a quick way to revisit all the different skills and see what you may need to review.
  • Open any mini-lesson and try the Overview to review the basics. Turn on Audio Coaching to listen while you read.
  • In the mini-lesson explore the Try It choices. These are step-by-step tutorials with feedback and hints. Or, try playing a Challenge Game to see what you know.
  • Click on the breadcrumbs (up in the purple banner) to go back to the Module page and choose a different mini-lesson.

Other ways to go to JUICE learning resources:

  • Go back to a favorite module or mini-lesson later: Click Home (in the purple banner), then Recently Visited for a list of modules and mini-lessons you worked in last; "orange slices" show details.

How can I get technical support?

Some temporary display issues in JUICE interactions and games may be corrected by clicking the Reload button on your browser.

If a JUICE page will not load or you are unable to continue using an Overview, Try It, or Challenge Game, please contact your institution's technical support team to report the issue.

What is JUICE?

JUICE is a new set of personalized online learning resources with friendly, interactive ways to review and practice academic skills. You can start anywhere and explore the choices to get as much help as you need. JUICE tracks your progress so you know what you've practiced and can easily pick up where you left off. You can browse or return to recently visited lessons from JUICE Home.

Which browsers does JUICE support?

Chrome is the recommended browser for JUICE. Firefox and Safari are also supported. Internet Explorer is not officially supported, so all features may not work as designed.

For best results:

  • Use one of these supported browsers.
  • Allow pop-ups from www.juiceyourskills.com.
  • For many types of errors, clicking Reload in your browser’s toolbar may fix the issue.
  • Remember to log out, so your information remains private, especially when using a public or shared computer.

Does JUICE work on PCs and Macintosh computers? What about tablets and smartphones?

JUICE runs in the web browser on PC and Mac computers but does not yet fully support mobile devices, such as tablets or smartphones, although features may work.

Does JUICE require any plug-ins or special apps?

No, JUICE works using standard HTML technology provided in your web browser. For best results:

  • Use a supported browser (see above).
  • Allow pop-ups from www.juiceyourskills.com.
  • For many types of errors, clicking Reload in your browser’s toolbar may fix the issue.
  • Remember to log out, so your information remains private, especially when using a public or shared computer.

How can I send feedback about JUICE?

We welcome your ideas and suggestions on how to improve JUICE. Click the vertical feedback tab (lower left of any page), type your feedback, and send.

Can I bookmark a favorite JUICE module or mini-lesson?

Not yet, but you can easily go back to modules and mini-lessons. Click Home (in the purple banner), then see the Recently Visited view, which shows what you worked on last. (We plan to add a “favorites” feature to JUICE soon.)

Feedback is welcome!

We appreciate suggestions and all kinds of feedback on how to make JUICE a helpful academic skill-building resource for all college students.

To send us your feedback, click Click the vertical feedback tab (lower left of any page), type your feedback, and send.
