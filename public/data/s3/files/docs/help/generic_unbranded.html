What is this site?

This site contains a collection of interactive skill-building resources that are designed to help refresh your academic skills and build confidence. We provide friendly, interactive ways to review and practice skills in real world situations.

From a module page, start with the page tour, then explore mini-lessons on different skills, or play the Challenge Wheel game to find out what you need to review.

See More...

Each module includes mini-lessons on a particular set of skills.

The mini-lessons provide:

  • A self-paced Overview that covers the basics and includes an audio coaching option for listening as you read
  • Step-by-step interactive Try It examples that walk you through how to apply the skill and give detailed feedback and hints
  • Short Challenge Games that include hints and feedback, a Show Answer option to keep you going, and cheers when you finish the game!

Support for accessibility

The main site pages and the Overview and Try it sections of each mini-lesson support keyboard navigation and screen readers. Challenge games and some of the other site pages are not yet accessible.

IMPORTANT! The site's keyboard navigation assumes that the user will be using the tab key on both Windows and the Mac to move from one area of the page to the next. Keyboard navigation has not been optimized for other forms of screen navigation.

Getting started

From a module page:

  • Click Page Tour (upper right in the banner) for a quick introduction to features.
  • Open a mini-lesson and try the Overview to see if you need to review the skill. To go back, use the bread crumbs (in the purple banner).
  • Click Audio Coaching (speaker icon) to listen as you read.
  • Try playing the Challenge Wheel game. It's a quick way to revisit all the different skills and see what you may need to review.

See More...

Other ways to access learning resources:

  • Go back to a favorite module or mini-lesson later: Click Home (in the purple banner), then Recently Visited for a list of modules and mini-lessons you worked in last; "orange slices" show details.
  • Search for a specific topic: Enter a word or phrase in the Search bar (in the upper right of the purple banner) and click the magnifying glass. You'll be given a list of lessons and resources most relevant to your search.

What is this site?

This is a new set of online learning resources with friendly, interactive ways to review and practice basic academic skills needed for success in college-level work.

How is this different from other skill-building resources?

This resource puts skill review and practice in a real world context and gives choices to help many different students find the right kind of help and get just enough review and practice to reach mastery. It is very interactive, self-directed, and provides detailed feedback, hints and encouragement -- so everyone will try practicing. Challenge Games may help motivate students to practice -– even when they really don’t like a particular subject.

Can I send feedback or suggestions for improvments?

We welcome your thoughts and suggestions. In the site, click the feedback tab on the lower left of any page, type your feedback in the box, and send.

Can I bookmark a favorite module or mini-lesson?

Not yet, but you can easily go back to modules and mini-lessons. Click Home (in the purple banner), then see the Recently Visited view, which highlights what you worked on last. (We plan to add a “favorites” feature soon.)

What browsers are supported?

Chrome is the recommended browser, but Firefox and Safari are also supported. Internet Explorer is not officially supported, so all features may not work as designed.

Are PCs and Macintosh computers supported? What about tablets and smartphones?

The site runs in the web browser on PC and Mac computers but does not yet fully support mobile devices, such as tablets or smartphones, although features may work.

Are any plug-ins or special apps required?

No, the site works using standard HTML technology provided in your web browser. For best results:

  • Use a supported browser (see above).
  • Allow pop-ups from www.juiceyourskills.com.
  • For many types of errors, clicking Reload in your browser’s tool bar may fix the issue.
  • Remember to log out, so your information remains private, especially when using a public or shared computer.

Feedback is welcome!

We appreciate suggestions and all kinds of feedback on how to make the site a helpful academic skill-building resource for all college students.

To send us your feedback, click Click the vertical feedback tab (lower left of any page), type your feedback, and send.
