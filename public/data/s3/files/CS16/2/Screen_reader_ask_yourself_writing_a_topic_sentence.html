Ask yourself: Writing a topic sentence

First, ask yourself, what do I want to write about?

This is your topic. An example is, "Cooking classes in public schools."

Next, ask yourself, what do I want to say?

This is your main idea. An example is, "This is a good idea for students in the third grade and above."

The topic and the main idea together make up the topic sentence.

You may add additional details that help your reader understand what you will write about in the paragraph. An example of a topic sentence is, "Providing cooking classes in public schools for students in the third grade and above will help students develop skills that relate to math, nutrition, and independent living."

Once you have your topic sentence, ask yourself, is my topic sentence giving the reader enough information?

If you answer no, your topic sentence needs to be revised.

If you answer yes, ask yourself:

Can I prove, show, or explain my ideas?

If you answer no, your topic sentence needs to be revised.

If you answer yes, you have a good topic sentence!
