Ask yourself: Deciding what support to use

You need to decide what supporting details to include in your paragraph. Try asking yourself these questions to decide if a supporting detail should be included.

Will this supporting detail tell my reader more about my topic sentence and prove, show, or explain the main idea?

If you answer no, this detail will not support your topic and shouldn't be included.

If you answer yes, then ask:

Will this supporting detail convince my audience that I am correct?

If you answer no, this detail will not support your topic and shouldn't be included.

If you answer yes, then ask:

Is my supporting detail accurate and based on facts, statistics, or examples?

If you answer no, this detail will not support your topic and shouldn't be included.

If you answered yes to all three questions, the supporting detail will support your topic!
