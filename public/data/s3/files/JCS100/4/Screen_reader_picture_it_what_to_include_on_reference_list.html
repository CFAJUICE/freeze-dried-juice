Deciding what to include on your list of references

For each source included in an in-text citation in your document, you need to include an entry on a separate list of references at the end of your document. To decide what sources to include on your list of references:

Review the in-text citations that you included in your writing.

Each source that is mentioned in an in-text citation should have a corresponding entry on your list of references, except for personal communications.

Your list of references should not include sources you read but did not use in your writing.

When you cite the same source multiple times, you only need one corresponding reference list entry.

Examples

Your writing has in-text citations for the sources Andrews, 2016 and Baker, 2013. Your list of references should have an entry for each of those sources.

Your writing includes a citation from D. Weber, which is information from a personal communication. It shouldn't be included on your list of references because it is a personal communication.

Your writing includes a second citation from Andrews, 2016. This source is already included on your list of references, so it doesn't need to be added again.

You read the source Maztosi, 2008 but didn't cite it in your writing. This source should not be included on your list of references.
